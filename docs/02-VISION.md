# MomConnect Platform — Vision & Mission

> Working title: **MomConnect** (client to choose final name)

## The Problem

Mothers are underresourced. The tools they need are scattered across dozens of disconnected apps and websites:

- They sell on Facebook Marketplace and Etsy (not designed for them)
- They find playdates on random Facebook groups (no structure, no safety)
- They search for DV resources on government websites (cold, hard to navigate, not private)
- They look for business mentorship on LinkedIn (not built for moms' schedules or realities)
- They piece together wellness from YouTube, meditation apps, and self-help books (no community, no continuity)

**There is no single place where a mother can be her whole self** — entrepreneur, caregiver, community member, survivor, healer — and find support for all of it.

## The Vision

**One account. One identity. Five connected spaces.**

Like Google has Gmail, Drive, Maps, and YouTube all under one account — MomConnect is an ecosystem of purpose-built websites, each excellent on its own, but powerful together because they share:

- **One account** (sign in once, access everything)
- **One profile** (your identity travels with you)
- **One community** (the mom you meet at a playdate might also be your next business partner)
- **One trust system** (reputation and reviews carry across sites)

## The Five Spaces

| Space | Purpose | Core Feeling |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| **Market** | Buy, sell, trade — crafts, baked goods, clothes, services | "I can earn and provide" |
| **Safe Space** | DV resources, AI safety planning, hotlines, escape support | "I am not alone, and there is a way out" |
| **Launch** | Entrepreneurship, mentorship, collaboration, AI business tools | "I can build something" |
| **Village** | Playdates, local events, travel connections, mom-to-mom networking | "I have my people" |
| **Restore** | Affirmations, meditations, prayers, boundary guides, healing audio | "I can breathe and come back to myself" |

## Guiding Principles

1. **Safety first** — Especially for Safe Space, but across the whole platform. Privacy, anonymity options, and careful data handling are non-negotiable.

2. **Made by moms, for moms** — The client is creating much of the healing content herself. The platform should amplify mom-created content, not replace it with generic AI.

3. **Local + global** — Many features are hyper-local (marketplace, playdates, DV shelters) but the community is global (a mom moving to a new city should find her people instantly).

4. **AI as assistant, not replacement** — AI helps with safety planning, business ideation, and resource discovery. It does not replace human connection, human mentorship, or human warmth.

5. **One thing at a time** — Each space should be simple and excellent on its own. A mom in crisis shouldn't have to navigate a complex mega-app. She should find exactly what she needs.

6. **Sustainability** — The platform needs a business model that doesn't exploit its users. Marketplace transaction fees, premium courses, and optional subscriptions — not surveillance advertising.

## What Success Looks Like

- A mom in a DV situation finds Safe Space, gets connected to local resources and an AI-guided escape plan, and later — when she's ready — discovers the entrepreneur hub and starts a small business selling her crafts on the marketplace.
- A stay-at-home mom organizes a playdate through Village, meets another mom who's starting a bakery, they collaborate through Launch, and she starts selling cupcakes on Market.
- A mom feeling overwhelmed opens Restore at 2am, listens to an affirmation the client recorded, and feels seen.
